# PART II - THE ZAMBIA CYBER SECURITY AGENCY

## 3. Establishment of Zambia Cyber Security Agency

<ol type="1">
  <li>There is established the Zambia Cyber Security Agency in the Office of the President which is responsible for the administration of this Act under the general direction of the President.</li>
  <li>The Agency shall be responsible for the coordination of cyber security matters in the Republic.</li>
  <li>The Agency shall collaborate with relevant institutions which are constitutionally mandated to defend the Republic in cyber warfare and offensive cyber operations to uphold the sovereignty of the Republic.</li>
</ol>

## 4. Functions of Agency

The functions of the Agency are to—
<ol type="a">
  <li>subject to the Zambia Security Intelligence Service Act, 1998, coordinate activities relating to cyber security and cyber resilience;</li>
  <li>take measures in response to cyber security incidents which may threaten critical information, critical information infrastructure or any information or infrastructure in the Republic likely to be affected by a cyber security incident;</li>
  <li>disseminate information on cyber threats and vulnerabilities;</li>
  <li>identify and ensure the protection of critical information and critical information infrastructure;</li>
  <li>establish codes of practice and standards for cyber security and monitor compliance with the codes of practice and standards by controllers;</li>
  <li>issue licences for the provision of cyber security services;</li>
  <li>regulate the conduct of cyber security service providers;</li>
  <li>promote and undertake research and development relating to cyber security;</li>
  <li>promote and undertake capacity building, education and awareness activities on matters relating to cyber security;</li>
  <li>undertake information security audits on critical information and critical information infrastructure;</li>
  <li>adopt standards for cyber security products and services and certify cyber security products and services;</li>
  <li>develop and implement a national cyber security response plan;</li>
  <li>undertake digital forensics;</li>
  <li>provide technical assistance and collaborate with other relevant national and international institutions in matters relating to this Act; and</li>
  <li>advise the President on matters relating to cyber security.</li>
</ol>

## 5. Director-General and other staff

<ol type="1">
  <li>The President shall, appoint a Director-General of the Agency who shall be a public officer.</li>
  <li>The Director-General is the chief executive officer of the Agency and is responsible for the day to day management of the Agency.</li>
  <li>The President shall appoint a Deputy Director-General, Directors and Deputy Directors who are necessary for the implementation of the provisions of this Act.</li>
  <li>The Director-General shall, on the recommendation of the Staff Board, appoint other officers below the rank of Deputy Director that are necessary for the implementation of the provisions of this Act.</li>
</ol>
